## ūüß¨ The Problem

<table>
<tr>
<td width="60%">

Pancreatic cancer is among the **deadliest malignancies** in oncology. Unlike many cancers, it offers almost no early warning ‚ÄĒ by the time symptoms appear, the disease has almost always spread.

The core challenge is radiological: pancreatic tumors appear **nearly iso-dense** against the surrounding soft tissue in CT scans. They sit deep inside complex abdominal anatomy, making them extraordinarily difficult to delineate manually.

**Manual 3D CT segmentation is:**
- ūüēź Extremely time-consuming per patient
- ‚ĚĆ Prone to inter-observer variability
- ūüďČ Clinically infeasible at scale

</td>
<td width="40%" align="center">

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|:-----:|
| 5-Year Survival Rate | **< 12%** |
| Diagnosed at Stage III‚ÄďIV | **~80%** |
| Pancreas volume in CT | **0.5‚Äď1%** |
| Smallest detectable tumor | **~5 mm** |
| CT scans in training set | **281** |
